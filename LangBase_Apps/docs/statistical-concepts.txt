# Statistical Concepts

## Descriptive Statistics
- Mean: Average of a dataset.
- Median: Middle value when data is ordered.
- Mode: Most frequently occurring value.
- Variance: Measure of data spread.
- Standard Deviation: Square root of variance, indicates dispersion.
- Range: Difference between maximum and minimum values.

---

## Probability
- Random Variable: Variable whose values depend on outcomes of a random phenomenon.
- Probability Distribution: Function describing likelihood of outcomes.
- Normal Distribution: Bell-shaped curve, symmetric around mean.
- Binomial Distribution: Probability of success/failure in fixed trials.
- Poisson Distribution: Probability of events occurring in fixed intervals.

---

## Inferential Statistics
- Population vs Sample: Entire group vs subset used for analysis.
- Sampling Methods: Random, stratified, cluster, systematic.
- Confidence Interval: Range of values likely to contain population parameter.
- Hypothesis Testing: Procedure to test assumptions about population.
  - Null Hypothesis (H₀): No effect or difference.
  - Alternative Hypothesis (H₁): Presence of effect or difference.
- p-value: Probability of observing results if H₀ is true.
- Significance Level (α): Threshold for rejecting H₀ (commonly 0.05).

---

## Correlation & Regression
- Correlation Coefficient (r): Strength and direction of linear relationship.
- Simple Linear Regression: Predict dependent variable using one independent variable.
- Multiple Regression: Predict dependent variable using multiple independent variables.
- R² (Coefficient of Determination): Proportion of variance explained by model.

---

## Advanced Concepts
- ANOVA (Analysis of Variance): Compare means across multiple groups.
- Chi-Square Test: Test independence of categorical variables.
- t-Test: Compare means between two groups.
- F-Test: Compare variances between groups.
- Bayesian Statistics: Update probabilities with new evidence.
- Central Limit Theorem: Distribution of sample means approaches normal as sample size increases.

---

## Data Considerations
- Bias: Systematic error in data collection or analysis.
- Outliers: Extreme values that may distort results.
- Missing Data: Strategies include imputation, deletion, or modeling.
- Overfitting: Model fits training data too closely, fails to generalize.
- Underfitting: Model too simple, fails to capture patterns.
